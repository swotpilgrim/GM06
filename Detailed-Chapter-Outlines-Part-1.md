# DETAILED CHAPTER OUTLINES: The Lighthouse Keeper (Chapters 1-15)

## Chapter 1: Arrival

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words
**POV:** Third person limited, Claire's perspective
**Setting:** Frank's fishing boat approaching Whitmore Point Lighthouse, Maine coast
**Time:** Late afternoon, late October 1981

**Purpose:** Establish characters, setting, isolation, and initial atmospheric unease as research team arrives at lighthouse island.

**Beats:**
- Frank's diesel trawler cuts through fog approaching the two-acre island, lighthouse visible as white tower. Team introduces themselves through work: Claire organizing equipment, Kate checking survey gear, Brian photographing with 35mm camera, Allison shooting atmospheric fog shots.
- Dock at high tide, unload supplies and research equipment. Frank is minimally helpful, taciturn, wants to leave quickly. Brief exchange about pickup in seven days, weather warnings.
- First exploration of island: tour the lighthouse exterior (65-foot tower, automated beacon), keeper's cottage (weathered grey, two-story), equipment shed. Claire assigns preliminary tasks, establishes camp routine.
- Frank departs into fog. Boat engine fades. Group realizes absolute isolation—no phone, no radio contact except marine emergency band, closest land three miles away.
- Sunset: lighthouse beacon activates, rhythmic rotation every fifteen seconds. Team carries final supplies to cottage. Allison feels watched, mentions it casually. Kate dismisses it. First night on the island begins with sound of waves and foghorn.

**Dialogue Topics:** Introductions and roles, Frank's warnings about weather and tides, logistics of seven-day stay, initial observations about lighthouse history (Brian), practical concerns about generator and supplies (Kate), atmospheric comments (Allison).

**Emotional Arc:** Anticipation and professional focus shifting to subtle unease. Claire projects confidence masking concern about isolation. Group bonding begins through shared work.

**Key Details:** Establish 1981 period details (film cameras, cassette recorders, marine radio, no cell phones). Frank's character establishes local knowledge and reluctance. Lighthouse automated 1966. Island geography: rocky, sparse vegetation, cliff on west side.

**Dialogue Topics:** Speculation about cottage inhabitant, debate between rational explanations versus concerning implications, Coast Guard protocols, Brian's knowledge of lighthouse automation history, security measures, watch rotation assignments.

**Emotional Arc:** Discovery excitement turns to discomfort and low-level fear. Professional researchers confronting evidence that doesn't fit expectations. Claire's authority establishing through calm response.

**Key Details:** Fresh food establishes timeline (current inhabitant), bed evidence (someone sleeping here regularly), cottage layout (two-story, multiple rooms, compromised security), group dynamic forming through crisis response.

## Chapter 6: The Drowning of Silas Grant

**Dialogue Topics:** Newspaper account of drowning, funeral details, Frank's potential testimony, speculation about identity if not Silas, radio antenna sabotage analysis, maritime signal meanings (three blasts = distress), isolation acceptance, rationing supplies discussion.

**Emotional Arc:** Brief hope from historical answer (Silas is dead, therefore someone else) crushed by communication loss. Isolation becoming absolute. Group acceptance they must survive four more days until Frank's scheduled return.

**Key Details:** November 1967 drowning specifics (storm, body recovery timeline, funeral), newspaper clipping as physical evidence, Frank identified in funeral photo, antenna cutting technical details (cable type, clean cut vs. damage), foghorn impossible operation.
